# Hero Content Blueprint — Sikkha (শিক্ষা বাংলা)

> **Role:** Product Copywriter / Growth Strategist / UX Writer
> **Scope:** Content strategy only — words, messages, information blocks
> **Constraint:** No UI, no code, no design, no visual discussion
> **Language:** Bengali (Bangla) — the platform's primary language
> **Audience:** Bangladeshi students Class 6–HSC, parents, teachers

---

## Part 1: Hero Content Block Architecture

The Hero contains exactly **7 content blocks**. Each block has one job. No block is decorative. No block is optional (unless marked).

---

### Block 1: Trust Badge

**What it is:** A single line of text above the headline that establishes credibility before the visitor reads anything else.

**Why it exists:** The visitor's first unconscious question is "Is this legitimate?" The badge answers that question before the headline is even read. It primes the visitor to trust what follows.

**What visitor question does it answer:** "Can I trust this platform?"

**Is it essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL. Without it, the headline lacks authority. A claim from an unknown platform carries less weight than a claim from a platform that has already proven itself.

**What should come before it:** Nothing. This is the first content block the visitor encounters (after the logo in the header).

**What should come after it:** The headline. The badge sets up trust; the headline delivers the promise.

---

### Block 2: Headline

**What it is:** The single most important sentence on the entire platform. It must communicate the core value proposition in under 10 words.

**Why it exists:** The headline is the gate. If it fails, nothing below it matters. The visitor decides within 2 seconds whether to keep reading or leave. The headline must answer "Is this for me?" and "Why should I care?" simultaneously.

**What visitor question does it answer:** "What is this and why should I care?"

**Is it essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL. This is non-negotiable. The headline is the highest-impact content on the page.

**What should come before it:** The trust badge (establishes credibility).

**What should come after it:** The supporting sentence (explains the headline's promise).

---

### Block 3: Supporting Sentence

**What it is:** One sentence that expands on the headline. It provides the specific details the headline intentionally omits for brevity.

**Why it exists:** The headline makes a promise; the supporting sentence explains how the platform delivers on that promise. It answers "How?" after the headline answers "What?" and "Why?"

**What visitor question does it answer:** "How does this platform help me achieve what the headline promises?"

**Is it essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL. Without it, the headline is a claim without evidence. The supporting sentence grounds the promise in reality.

**What should come before it:** The headline (makes the promise).

**What should come after it:** The primary CTA (invites action).

---

### Block 4: Primary CTA

**What it is:** The single most important action the visitor can take. One button, one destination, one clear outcome.

**Why it exists:** The Hero's entire purpose is to get the visitor to take one action. The primary CTA is that action. It must be the lowest-friction, highest-value action available.

**What visitor question does it answer:** "What should I do next?"

**Is it essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL. Without a CTA, the Hero is a billboard — it informs but doesn't convert.

**What should come before it:** The supporting sentence (explains what the visitor gets).

**What should come after it:** The secondary CTA (an alternative for visitors not ready for the primary action).

---

### Block 5: Secondary CTA

**What it is:** An alternative action for visitors who aren't ready for the primary CTA. It should be lower-commitment and lower-friction.

**Why it exists:** Not every visitor is ready for the primary action. Some need more information before committing. The secondary CTA captures these visitors instead of losing them.

**What visitor question does it answer:** "Is there a lower-risk way to explore this platform?"

**Is it essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL for conversion. Without it, visitors who aren't ready for the primary CTA have nowhere to go except "leave."

**What should come before it:** The primary CTA (captures high-intent visitors).

**What should come after it:** The trust message (reassures visitors who are still deciding).

---

### Block 6: Trust Message

**What it is:** A single line of social proof or credibility evidence that appears near the CTAs.

**Why it exists:** The visitor is about to take action (or not). The trust message provides the final reassurance they need. It answers "Am I making the right choice?"

**What visitor question does it answer:** "Are other people using this? Is it proven?"

**Is it essential or optional:** ESSENTIAL for conversion. Trust messages near CTAs increase click-through by 15-30% in EdTech.

**What should come before it:** The secondary CTA (the visitor has seen both actions).

**What should come after it:** Nothing within the Hero. The trust message is the final content block before the visitor scrolls or acts.

---

### Block 7: Urgency Message (Conditional)

**What it is:** A time-sensitive message that creates motivation to act now rather than later. Only appears when relevant (exam season).

**Why it exists:** Urgency is the #1 conversion driver in EdTech. Students procrastinate. A deadline (exam date) converts procrastination into action.

**What visitor question does it answer:** "Why should I start now instead of later?"

**Is it essential or optional:** CONDITIONAL. Essential during exam season (2-3 months before board exams). Optional otherwise. When active, it should appear as part of the trust message or as a standalone line.

**What should come before it:** The CTAs (the visitor has seen the action options).

**What should come after it:** The trust message (urgency + trust together create maximum conversion pressure).

**Winner: Option 1 — "ফ্রি শুরু করুন"**

**Why it wins:**
- **Clarity:** Two words. "ফ্রি" (free) + "শুরু করুন" (start). The visitor knows exactly what happens when they click: they start, and it's free.
- **Conversion:** "ফ্রি" is the highest-converting word in the Bengali EdTech vocabulary. It eliminates the #1 barrier (cost) in the CTA itself. The visitor doesn't need to wonder "will I be charged?" — the CTA answers that question.
- **Trust:** "ফ্রি" is a promise. If the platform delivers free content after this click, trust increases. If it doesn't, trust is destroyed. The CTA is honest.
- **Simplicity:** 2 words. The shortest possible CTA that communicates both action and price.
- **BN Suitability:** "ফ্রি" is universally understood in Bangladesh (English loanword commonly used in Bengali context). "শুরু করুন" is formal Bengali appropriate for an educational platform.

**Why others lose:**
- Option 5 ("বিনামূল্যে শুরু করুন") uses "বিনামূল্যে" which is 3 syllables vs "ফ্রি" which is 1 syllable. In a CTA, brevity wins.
- Option 9 ("নিবন্ধন করুন" = Register) asks for commitment before demonstrating value. The visitor hasn't seen content yet — asking them to register is premature.
- Option 4 ("বোর্ড প্রশ্ন দেখুন") is specific but limits the visitor to one content type. The primary CTA should be broad enough for all visitor types.

**Winner: Option 1 — "SSC পরীক্ষা: ১৮০ দিন বাকি — এখনই প্রস্তুতি শুরু করুন"**

**Why it wins:**
- **Clarity:** Specific exam (SSC) + specific time (180 days) + specific action (start preparation now). No ambiguity.
- **Conversion:** Specificity creates urgency. "১৮০ দিন বাকি" (180 days remaining) is more urgent than "পরীক্ষা কাছে আসছে" (exam is approaching). The visitor can count the days — it's real.
- **Trust:** The countdown is a fact, not a claim. The visitor can verify it against the official exam schedule.
- **Simplicity:** Three parts: [exam] + [time] + [action]. Clear structure.
- **BN Suitability:** "SSC" is universally understood. "দিন বাকি" (days remaining) is natural Bengali.

**Important note:** The urgency message is CONDITIONAL. It should only appear when an exam is within 6 months. The specific exam (SSC/HSC) and the number of days should be dynamically determined based on the current date and exam schedule. The example above uses SSC with 180 days as a template.

**Why others lose:**
- Option 2 ("HSC exam is coming soon") is vague — "শীঘ্রই" (soon) doesn't create the same urgency as a specific number.
- Option 3 ("How many days until your exam?") is a question, not a statement — questions create thinking, not action.
- Option 8 ("Your competitors have already started") uses social pressure, which can backfire — it may demotivate rather than motivate.

### Why Each Block Is in Its Position

| Position | Block | Reason |
|----------|-------|--------|
| 1st | Trust Badge | Must answer "can I trust this?" BEFORE the visitor reads the headline. Primes credibility. |
| 2nd | Headline | The promise. Must immediately follow the trust badge to benefit from the credibility priming. |
| 3rd | Supporting Sentence | Explains the headline's promise. Must follow the headline to provide context. |
| 4th | Primary CTA | The action. Must follow the supporting sentence so the visitor knows what they're getting before being asked to act. |
| 5th | Secondary CTA | The alternative. Must follow the primary CTA to capture visitors not ready for the primary action. |
| 6th | Trust Message | Social proof. Must appear near the CTAs to reassure the visitor at the moment of decision. |
| 7th | Urgency Message | Time pressure. Must appear last because urgency amplifies everything before it — it makes the headline more compelling, the CTAs more urgent, and the trust message more reassuring. |

### Content Decisions Log

| Decision | Choice | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Headline focuses on outcome (A+) not feature (lectures) | Outcome > Feature | Students want results, not tools. "A+" is the result; "lectures" is the tool. |
| "80% free" appears twice (badge + supporting sentence) | Strategic repetition | The badge answers "is it free?" instantly. The supporting sentence reinforces it with context. This is not duplication — it's layered communication. |
| Primary CTA is "Start for free" not "Register" | Low-friction > High-commitment | "Register" asks for personal information. "Start for free" asks for nothing. The visitor can explore without committing. |
| Secondary CTA is "See board questions" not "See premium" | Free > Paid | The secondary CTA should be lower-commitment than the primary. "See premium" is higher-commitment (it's a sales page). "See board questions" is lower-commitment (it's free content). |
| Trust message uses numbers not claims | Data > Opinion | "10,000+ students" is a fact. "Most trusted platform" is an opinion. Facts are more credible. |
| Urgency message is conditional, not permanent | Dynamic > Static | Permanent urgency ("start now!") loses credibility. Dynamic urgency (exam countdown) is factual and self-verifying. |
| No "premium" mentioned in Hero | Free-first > Premium-first | Premium is a revenue concern, not a conversion concern. The Hero's job is to convert visitors to users. Revenue comes after value demonstration. |
| Supporting sentence mentions "government curriculum" | Credibility > Features | "Government curriculum" is the strongest trust signal in Bangladeshi education. It's more powerful than listing content types. |
